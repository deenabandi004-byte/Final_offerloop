# TikTok Cold Restart Plan

The brief: 73,000 dormant followers, no recent posts to ride, and the 2026 algorithm is harsher on returning accounts than it was in 2024. This file is the plan.

Compiled by Claude (Opus 4.7), May 2026. WebSearch + WebFetch only (no Perplexity MCP). Sources cited inline.

## Part 1: What the 2026 algorithm does to a dormant account

This is the harshest finding in the research. From [Socialync's 2026 algorithm update](https://www.socialync.io/blog/tiktok-algorithm-2026-what-works-now) and [Ampfluence's 2026 deep-dive](https://www.ampfluence.com/how-the-tiktok-algorithm-works-in-2026-and-how-to-beat-it-for-more-reach/):

1. **Follower-first testing model.** Your video is shown to a small slice of your existing followers first. If completion / shares / saves are strong, the video gets pushed wider. **If your followers don't engage, the video dies.** For Offerloop, this means the 73K dormant followers are a *gate*, not just an asset. If they don't watch through the first post, the post never escapes.

2. **70% completion rate is the new viral threshold** in 2026 (up from ~50% in 2024). If you lose half your audience in the first two seconds, you're not getting reach.

3. **Reach takes months to rebuild after dormancy.** "An account could go dormant and come back viral before. Now, reach levels take months to rebuild." Accounts posting below 1x/week lose algorithmic momentum.

4. **Consistency beats intensity.** [Multiple 2026 sources](https://joinbrands.com/blog/how-often-to-post-on-tiktok/) report that creators who post consistently at the same times see 40% higher growth rates than sporadic posters.

5. **First-hour engagement matters more.** Reply to every comment within the first hour to boost the algorithm signal.

The good news, balancing the bad:

- TikTok's algorithm re-tests dormant content over time. [Videos can go viral 3 weeks to several months after posting](https://www.tiktok.com/discover/why-did-my-tiktok-go-viral-months-after-it-had-posted) if completion / retention signals stay strong.
- [YouTube CEO Susan Wojcicki publicly stated in 2019](https://variety.com/2019/digital/news/youtube-ceo-to-burned-out-creators-its-ok-to-take-a-break-from-posting-1203412088/) that creators returning from hiatus had *more* views on average than before the break, based on data across millions of channels and hundreds of break durations. **Not TikTok data, not 2026 data**, but directionally encouraging that dormant audiences are reactivatable when content is good.

**Implication for Offerloop**: the 73K followers are still a real asset, BUT the first post has to be ruthlessly tuned for completion rate (not for the comeback narrative). If you make a "we're back" video that hits 30% completion, you've signaled to the algorithm that your audience doesn't care, and you've made every subsequent post harder to reach.

## Part 2: The "address the absence" question

**TL;DR: don't address it in the first video. Address it in a pinned comment, and address it in post 4 or 5.**

Reasoning:

- I could not find a clean head-to-head study comparing "acknowledge gap" vs. "just resume" with metrics. **DATA THIN.**
- What I did find: the "we're back" / "we're so back" hashtag culture is widespread but [TheSocialCat warns](https://thesocialcat.com/glossary/we-re-so-back) the phrase is overused, and audiences "stop caring" when it loops.
- Most established creators who return from a 3+ month gap do address it, often in the first 10 seconds. But they earn the right to do so because *they themselves* are the audience's reason for watching. Offerloop's audience does not follow because they care about Sid, Nick, and Rylan personally; they follow because of the recruiting niche. So the audience-care vector is different.
- The 2026 algorithm gate (follower-first testing) punishes any video where the first 1.5 seconds don't grab. "Hey guys, we're back" is the slowest possible hook.

**Recommendation**: First post is pure content, hook-tested, recruiting-niche, signed off by one founder. Pinned comment under the first post says one line: "We've been heads-down rebuilding for the past few weeks. Going to be posting here every day for the next 30 days. Tell us in the comments who you'd most like to land at this summer." This addresses the absence, primes engagement, and lets the algorithm see comments rolling in.

## Part 3: Post #1 concept and script

**Owner**: Nick (CEO). Reasoning: Nick is the public face per the `offerloop-90-day-plan.md` direction, and the algorithm needs one identifiable human in the niche so it can categorize the account.

**Setting**: Nick alone, phone-shot, well-lit, no production gloss. Vertical, 9:16. Length target: 28 to 35 seconds.

**Format**: Talking-head intercut with screen recordings of an actual cold email + an actual reply.

**Hook (0 to 1.5 seconds)**:
> Visual: Nick on camera holds up his phone showing a Goldman Sachs rejection email.
> Audio: "Goldman rejected me sophomore year."

This is a pattern interrupt + curiosity gap. The viewer's brain wants to know "and then what?" Pattern interrupt + curiosity gap are two of the three hook triggers [Conbersa's 2026 hook research](https://www.conbersa.ai/learn/best-tiktok-hooks) identifies.

**Beat 2 (1.5 to 7 seconds)**:
> Visual: Cut to Nick, slight zoom in.
> Audio: "So I sent 47 cold emails to their analysts. Three of them turned into Superdays."

**Beat 3 (7 to 22 seconds)**:
> Visual: Screen recording of the actual cold email Nick sent (use a real one from his sent folder, lightly redacted). Highlight the subject line, the opener, the ask. 4 to 5 second pauses on each.
> Audio: "Here's exactly how I wrote it. Subject line: [specific]. Opener: [specific]. Ask: one specific thing, 15 minute call, this Thursday at 4 PM."

**Beat 4 (22 to 30 seconds)**:
> Visual: Cut back to Nick.
> Audio: "I'm Nick. I'm a college senior. I built a tool that does this for you. Link in bio."

**Beat 5 (30 to 35 seconds)**:
> Visual: Quick screen capture of the Offerloop product (one screen, no scrolling).
> Audio: "First 100 students get free Elite. Comment your dream firm below."

**Caption** (under the video):
> "If your inbox is silent during recruiting season, the problem isn't you. It's the framework. Link in bio for the cold email tool we built."

**Pinned comment** (Nick posts this from his founder account immediately after publishing):
> "We've been heads-down rebuilding for the past month. Going to be posting daily for the next 30 days. Tell me below what firm you most want to land at this summer. I'll respond to every one."

**Hashtags** (in the caption, not the comment):
> #ibrecruiting #consultingrecruiting #collegerecruiting #coldemail #sophomoresummer

Why these hashtags: they are niche-specific (algorithm needs niche signals to re-categorize the dormant account) but each one has enough volume (500K to 5M views) to give the post a chance to escape.

**One important thing the algorithm needs**: high comment velocity in the first hour. The pinned comment ("comment your dream firm") is designed to seed this. Nick must respond to every comment in the first 60 minutes. Set a phone alarm.

## Part 4: Posts 2 through 7 (the first two weeks)

The principle: each post is niche-anchored to recruiting, each post features one of the three founders by name, each post has a different format so the algorithm sees variety within the niche.

**Post #2 (Day 2): Sid, technical / behind-the-scenes**

- Hook (0 to 1.5s): Sid at his laptop. "I'm the CTO of Offerloop. This is the prompt that writes 47 cold emails in 12 seconds."
- Format: Fast-cut screen recording showing the user pasting a LinkedIn URL, the AI generating an email, the user sending it. Captioned overlays for each step.
- Length: 22 to 28 seconds.
- CTA: "Link in bio. Free for students."
- Why: introduces Sid by name (algorithm gets a second human signal), shows the product directly, completion-rate optimized (visible motion every few seconds).

**Post #3 (Day 3): Rylan, listicle**

- Hook (0 to 1.5s): "Three reasons your cold email got ignored."
- Format: Rylan on camera, three captioned counter-examples shown as screenshots, fast-paced.
- Length: 30 to 40 seconds.
- CTA: "Comment which one you've done before."
- Why: explicitly asks for comments (algorithm signal), addresses a specific pain.

**Post #4 (Day 4): Nick, polarizing POV**

- Hook (0 to 1.5s): "OCR isn't broken. It's just broken for 80% of you."
- Format: Nick on camera, no screen overlays. Pure POV. Cite one data point.
- Length: 35 to 45 seconds.
- CTA: "Disagree? Comment why."
- Why: polarizing posts drive comment velocity. The OCR-is-rigged take is real and felt by your ICP.

**Post #5 (Day 5): Real user, testimonial**

- Hook (0 to 1.5s): Student on camera. "I had a 3.4 GPA from a non-target. I just got a Goldman SA offer."
- Format: User talks directly to camera. They explain what they did. They mention Offerloop by name once. No script-y "this app changed my life" language.
- Length: 30 to 45 seconds.
- CTA: "Tag a friend who needs to see this."
- Why: social proof + new face + the algorithm sees the niche reinforced. **CONDITIONAL**: requires getting one volunteer from the 91 churned paid users or from a current power user. If you can't find one by Day 5, swap this with a different format and queue the testimonial for Week 2.

**Post #6 (Day 6): Sid, proprietary data drop**

- Hook (0 to 1.5s): "We pulled 12,000 IB analyst LinkedIn profiles. Here's where they went to school."
- Format: Sid on camera intro, then 4 to 5 visual data slides (bar chart of school distribution, GPA range, etc.). Sid voiceover.
- Length: 35 to 45 seconds.
- CTA: "Want this for your dream firm? Link in bio."
- Why: only Offerloop can publish this kind of data; it's a defensible content moat. Builds credibility, not just installs.

**Post #7 (Day 7): Nick, address the absence directly**

- Hook (0 to 1.5s): "I'm not going to lie. Offerloop went down for a month."
- Format: Nick on camera, talking-head, sparingly cut. This is the "honest comeback" video, sized to ~45 seconds. Acknowledge the gap, name what broke, name what's different now, name what's coming.
- Honesty-to-hype ratio: 70/30 (per `cold-restart-research.md` Part 6).
- CTA: "If you signed up before and the site was down for you, comment below. I'll personally extend your Pro for 60 days."
- Why: by Day 7, you've earned the right to address the absence because you've shown 6 days of substantive content. Now the audience cares about you, not just the niche. The 60-day Pro extension is the Offerloop equivalent of CrowdStrike's $10 Uber Eats gift card: a concrete, named gesture that costs little and signals contrition.

## Part 6: "Do not do this" list

Based on patterns from `cold-restart-research.md` and 2026 algorithm sources:

1. **Don't open the first post with "Hey guys, we're back!"** The hook eats your completion rate and the algorithm gate fires. Acknowledge the absence in post #7, not post #1.
2. **Don't repost old content from before the outage.** Algorithm will treat it as low-effort.
3. **Don't switch niches.** If the 73K followers came for recruiting content, do not pivot the account to general college / lifestyle / dance content. The algorithm needs niche signals to recategorize.
4. **Don't post twice on the same day in Week 1.** Overwhelms the dormant followers, and the second post starves the first of algorithmic share.
5. **Don't use "follow for part 2" or comment-beg phrasing.** The 2026 algorithm explicitly down-ranks engagement-bait formulas.
6. **Don't use trending sounds that aren't recruiting-relevant.** Trending sound + off-niche content confuses the algorithm. Use original audio or a quiet background sound.
7. **Don't put the Offerloop logo in the first 1.5 seconds.** Logos kill completion rate.
8. **Don't disable comments.** Comments are the most-weighted engagement signal in 2026.
9. **Don't boost the first post with TikTok Promote or Spark Ads.** No paid boost should run before you have 5 organic posts of completion data. Paid boost before signal wastes spend.
10. **Don't go cinematic / pre-produced for post #1.** Raw phone-shot content outperforms produced content for returning accounts (signals you're "back to basics" and algorithmically reads as authentic). Save production for post #15 or later, once the account is re-warmed.
11. **Don't post on Saturday or Sunday for the first two weeks.** College students don't scroll TikTok during weekend mornings (they're hungover or out); Friday and Monday evenings are the spike windows.
12. **Don't ask all three founders to post on the same day.** Spread the founder appearances so the algorithm sees variety.
13. **Don't link directly in the video itself.** "Link in bio" is correct. Linking on screen looks try-hard and reads as ad.

## Part 7: Measurement

By end of Week 2, you want to see:

- **Average completion rate above 50%** on your top 3 posts (you're below the 70% viral threshold but climbing).
- **At least one post above 25K views** (anything below 10K means the follower gate didn't open).
- **Comment count averaging >20 per post** (signal that engagement velocity is reactivating).
- **Daily follower growth back to net positive** (you'll lose some dormant followers in the first week, that's fine; you should be net positive by Day 10).
- **Visible bio link clicks** (TikTok Analytics → Insights → Profile Views and Link Clicks).

If by Day 14 you don't see at least one post above 25K views, the hook formula is wrong. Rewrite from the Palmy "exam panic → AI → flex" template (in `marketing-research.md`) using a different recruiting pain.

If by Day 14 followers are still net-negative, **address it directly**: a Day 15 post saying "I lost a thousand of you over the last month. I deserved it. Here's what I'm going to post for the next 60 days." This is the second-chance honesty move.
