# Offerloop: 90-Day Founder-Led Growth Plan

For Nick (CEO), Sid (CTO), Rylan (CMO). Based on the research in `marketing-research.md`. Drafted May 2026.

Current state: $1,665 MRR, 91 paid users, 1,027 total signups, 13.4% lifetime paid conversion, $0 paid acquisition. Plan assumes that constraint holds.

---

## A. Pattern Recognition (ranked by frequency across Tier 1 + Tier 2)

These tactics showed up across the 15+ companies researched. Ranked by how many cases used them.

1. **Founder identity as the marketing channel.** Cluely (Roy Lee), Cal AI (Zach Yadegari), Rizz (Roman Khaves), Friend.com (Avi Schiffmann), Granola (Chris Pedregal), Linear (Karri Saarinen), Superhuman (Rahul Vohra), Wordware launches, Series (Nathaneo Johnson), Palmy. Almost every company.
2. **Concierge / one-by-one early onboarding.** Superhuman (200 by Rahul personally), Arc (Zoom demos per invite), Granola (stealth year), Notion (Ivan replying on Twitter), Cron (private beta), Linear (curated waitlist).
3. **High-volume, formulaic short-form video.** Cal AI (250 creators, daily output), Rizz (TikTok demos), Palmy (exam panic → AI → flex), Cluely (cinematic films but on a cadence).
4. **Free product or generous free tier as the bottom-up acquisition layer.** Notion (free for students), Linear (free indie tier), Granola, Cron (entirely free pre-acquisition), Raycast (free w/ Pro).
5. **One polarizing or counter-cultural take used as the launch frame.** Cluely (cheat on everything), Friend (loneliness is solvable by AI), Wordware (your Twitter says you're a loser), Linear (devtools are slow and badly designed).
6. **Ambassador / community programs run by a single named insider.** Notion Pros / Ambassadors (Ben Lang ran it), Delphi creator network, Cal AI's retainer roster.
7. **Build-in-public founder Twitter / X as a credible content channel.** Linear, Granola, Notion, Friend, Cluely (post-launch).
8. **Survey-on-signup that doubles as customer research.** Linear's hidden survey, Superhuman's PMF Engine, both producing better PMF and a steady supply of testimonials.
9. **Multi-launch over single launch.** Linear's "launched at company, beta, paid tier, Series A" framework. Most VC-backed analogs had at least 3 launch events that compounded.
10. **A free, shareable side tool that funnels into the main product.** Wordware (Twitter Personality → main platform). Less common but very high leverage when it works.

## B. What Translates to Offerloop, and What Doesn't

### Translates well

- **Founder identity is your single highest-leverage asset.** You are three college students selling to college students at the same schools you attend. That is identity congruence the older D2C analogs above (Granola, Particle, Friend) had to engineer. You already have it. Stop hiding behind the brand account. Specifically, Nick is the CEO and most natural "face." He needs to be on LinkedIn 5x/week and X 3x/week in his own voice.
- **The Palmy formula maps almost directly.** "Exam panic → AI cheat code → flex result" becomes "rejection panic → cold email → callback flex." Career recruiting has the same emotional shape as exams: high stakes, low information, age-anchored anxiety. The output that gets screenshotted is just as native to this audience.
- **Concierge onboarding on 91 paid users is achievable in 30 days.** Nick (or split with Rylan) can Zoom every paid user this quarter. Three calls a day for 30 days clears the queue.
- **Survey-on-signup is free and high signal.** Linear's "Why do you want this?" → tagged database is a 4-hour engineering build for Sid. Do it before you do anything else this week.
- **Ambassador / club president program scales founder-led growth.** Target schools have club presidents who are natural micro-influencers. They need: a) credibility to give to their club ("we have a partnership with Offerloop"), b) something free to hand out ("first 50 members get Pro for free"). You can give them both at zero cost.
- **Free shareable side tool.** This is the Wordware mechanic and it is the single most copy-able idea in the research file. See the Week 9 plan below.

### Does not translate

- **Cluely's "cheat" angle is brand poison for Offerloop.** Cluely sells the fantasy of bypassing the hard part of getting a job. Offerloop is the hard part, done better. If Offerloop ever leans on "cheat-coded your way into Goldman," every career office in the country will block you, every parent will distrust you, and recruiters will treat your users as red flags. **Do not chase Cluely's hook.** The audience overlap is real; the brand fit is wrong.
- **Cal AI's 250-creator retainer is impossible at zero budget.** The first $1M of Cal AI revenue came when the founder posted himself daily. That part is replicable. The acceleration from $1M to $50M was paid. Do not budget against that scale.
- **Friend.com / Schiffmann's $1.8M domain + subway ads.** No.
- **Waitlist-as-gating is a trap for Offerloop.** Arc, Superhuman, Cron used waitlists because they had real product / server / support constraints. You don't. If you put a college student on a waitlist for a recruiting tool, they will go use the next free thing and never come back. Recruiting urgency is too high for artificial scarcity.
- **Particle's "publisher partnership" launch is too slow for you.** It works for a news app because publishers are a small handful of decision-makers. For Offerloop the equivalent is school career offices, which are a B2B sale you should not start yet. Defer.
- **Product Hunt as a primary channel is a low-priority bet.** Notion, Cron, Raycast all hit PH top spots, but their audiences were SF tech operators. College recruits do not live on PH. List there, but don't engineer your launch around it.

### The honest part

You have one structural advantage and three structural problems.

The advantage: founder-audience identity match.

The problems:
1. Career recruiting is **seasonal**. The Palmy formula runs 365 days a year because exams happen all the time. Your viral demand peaks in early fall (IB OCR), late winter (consulting recruiting), and spring (SA placements). Plan content around these spikes, not against a uniform cadence.
2. Your audience is **embarrassed to talk about using your product**. Cal AI users will TikTok about losing weight. Your users will not TikTok about needing help writing cold emails. The shareable artifact has to be designed to make using Offerloop look like a *flex*, not a *crutch*.
3. **You have no founder content engine running right now.** All three of you need to ship content this week. The plan below treats this as the binding constraint.

## C. 90-Day Plan

Three 30-day phases, each with one strategic theme, named owners, and one measurable outcome.

**Theme**: Set up the engine, do not yet try to scale. Talk to every paying user, build the survey, get founder content posting.

**Outcome to measure**: (a) PMF Engine survey shipped, (b) all 91 paid users have had at least one personal touch from Nick, (c) each founder has posted at least 10 pieces of public content.

### Phase 2, Days 31 to 60: Distribution (Owners: Rylan lead, Nick amplify, Sid ship)

**Theme**: Scale the Palmy formula. Take the founder content engine and turn it into a 20-creator network.

**Outcome to measure**: (a) 100 pieces of formulaic short-form content posted, (b) at least 3 club partnerships running paid programming with co-branded landing pages, (c) at least one organic post exceeding 100K views.

**Tactic 1: The Palmy Formula, Applied**

Write the hook formula down. Hand it to Ambassadors. The structure for every post:

> Beat 1 (hook, ~3 seconds): "I had a 3.4 GPA at a non-target. I got rejected from every BB IB SA program."
>
> Beat 2 (the pivot, ~8 seconds): "Then I sent 47 cold emails using this Offerloop framework."
>
> Beat 3 (the flex, ~12 seconds): "I got 3 SA offers. Here's the exact email."
>
> CTA (~3 seconds): "Link in bio. Free if you're a student."

20 Ambassadors × 1 post / week × 4 weeks = 80 posts. If one in five hits 100K views (typical for this format on TikTok Career-Tok), that's 16 hits. Cal AI's early funnel was 10 installs per 1,000 views; even at half that rate, 16 × 100K × 0.5% = 8,000 installs.

**Tactic 2: Founder Two-Stage Launch (the Cluely move, sanitized)**

Cluely's playbook was: get earned reputation on one polarizing thing, *then* launch the product. Your sanitized version:

- Stage 1, Weeks 5 to 6: Nick publishes a single piece of polarizing content, not a product pitch. A LinkedIn essay or X thread titled something like: "OCR is a scam for the bottom 80% of college students" or "I'm a college CEO. Here are the 3 things career offices won't tell you about IB recruiting." Pure POV, no link to Offerloop. Goal: 5K reads.
- Stage 2, Week 7 to 8: relaunch a real product update (e.g., new Meeting Prep, a free side tool) with a cinematic short video, riding on top of the reputation Stage 1 just built. Nick's existing audience will amplify.

**Tactic 3: Club Activation**

The 5 partnerships from Phase 1 do real programming in Phase 2:

- Co-branded landing page per club: offerloop.ai/usc-ibsa, offerloop.ai/wharton-mbb
- One "lunch and learn" Zoom per club, hosted by Nick, 30 mins
- Each lunch-and-learn ends with a custom signup code that gives club members 6 months free Elite
- Each club gets a leaderboard ("USC IB Society members have sent 1,247 cold emails this week")

This is the Notion Ambassador program adapted for your audience.

**Phase 2 owner table**

| Lane | Owner | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Ambassador content QC | Rylan | Daily review of new Ambassador posts; share top performers in the Ambassador group chat |
| Founder LinkedIn / X | Nick | 5 posts/week (3 LinkedIn + 2 X) |
| Founder technical / "ship" content | Sid | 2 posts/week |
| Club partnership ops | Rylan | 2 new clubs signed per week |
| Concierge calls | Nick | 1/day (taper down from Phase 1) |
| Free side tool prototype | Sid | Spec by Day 45, working alpha by Day 60 |

**Kill criteria for Phase 2**: If no Ambassador post hits 100K views by Day 60, the formula is wrong (not the people). Rewrite the formula with three people who already make Career-Tok content, not the cold-recruited Ambassadors.

### Phase 3, Days 61 to 90: Compound (Owners: Sid lead, Nick public, Rylan systematize)

**Theme**: Ship a free shareable side tool (the Wordware mechanic) and use a real product launch as the spike event.

**Outcome to measure**: (a) Side tool shipped, with 25K+ uses in its first 2 weeks, (b) one real product launch event with coordinated content across all three founders + the Ambassador network, (c) MRR trajectory inflecting upward (target: $3.5K MRR by Day 90, double from today).

**Tactic 1: The Free Side Tool (Wordware mechanic)**

Sid builds and ships a free, no-login (or email-only) side tool. The tool is designed for one job: generate a personalized, shareable artifact that costs Offerloop very little compute but creates social currency for the user. Candidates:

- **"Brutal Resume Roast"**: Paste resume URL → 5-bullet "Here's why Goldman would reject you" output, with the option to "Fix this in Offerloop." Personalized, screenshot-bait, painful. Wordware's exact mechanic.
- **"Your School's Recruiting Reality"**: Pick your school + target firm (USC × Goldman). Output: "37 USC undergrads got SA offers at Goldman last cycle. Here are the 3 most common backgrounds. Here's the average # of cold emails they sent." Uses anonymized internal Offerloop data as the asset. Powerful because no competitor has the data.
- **"The Cold Email Anonymous Critique"**: Paste your draft cold email → AI returns a numbered list of what's wrong with it, with a side-by-side fix. Hyper-personalized, embarrassing-in-a-good-way, screenshot-able.

Recommendation (decisive call): build "Your School's Recruiting Reality" because it is the only one that requires data you uniquely have. The other two anyone with a GPT key can ship. This one is defensible.

**Tactic 2: The Coordinated Launch**

End of Phase 3 is one real launch event. Pick a substantive product milestone (Meeting Prep V2, the side tool itself, a public product partnership). On launch day:

- Nick: cinematic founder video (phone-shot is fine, doesn't have to be Cluely-budget; the *script* matters more than the production)
- Sid: technical thread on what shipped and why
- Rylan: coordinated Ambassador post wave (all 20 Ambassadors post the same day with their own personal hook)
- All 3: comment-amplify each other's posts within the first hour to prime the algorithm
- Product Hunt listing on the same day (for the SF / VC audience, not the primary funnel)

The launch builds on top of the Stage 1 + Stage 2 reputation you accumulated in Phase 2. Linear's multi-launch principle: each event is bigger than the last because the audience compounds.

**Tactic 3: Systematize What Worked**

Rylan owns this. End of Day 90, you need:

- A documented Ambassador handbook (what they get, what they post, how often, how to claim credit)
- A documented content cadence by founder (so it survives a busy week)
- A scorecard for the next quarter: MRR, paid signups, % from founder content, % from Ambassador content, % from side tool, % from clubs

You will not have all the patterns optimized in 90 days. You will have the *engine*.

## D. What to Stop Doing

In service of the plan, here are things to *not* do this quarter:

- Do not run a waitlist. You have nothing to gate.
- Do not buy paid social. Even small spends teach you the wrong lessons before the organic engine is calibrated.
- Do not start a B2B / school career office push. You will use 50% of one founder's brain on it and learn nothing. Park it until you hit $5K MRR.
- Do not do a Product Hunt as a "launch event" by itself. Use it as a side-amplifier on launch day, not as the centerpiece.
- Do not redesign the marketing site this quarter. The site is not the constraint. Founder content + Ambassador volume + the side tool is.
