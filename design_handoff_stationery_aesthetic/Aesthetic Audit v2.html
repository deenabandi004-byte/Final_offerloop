A second pass, after notes

Not another AI product.
A letter, opened.

v1 was correct but generic — warm paper, Geist, Fraunces, hairlines. Tasteful AI slop. v2 commits to a direction stationery. The language of a letter: cream paper, printer's ink, an engraved seal, a serif italic for the human moments, a hand-drawn underline as the single signature gesture. Free fonts only. No custom icon set. Realistic to ship.

I · Revisions from v1

What changed, and why.

Six revisions against v1, each responding to a specific concern: licensing cost, scalability, risk of looking amateur, and realistic ROI on custom iconography.

Fixed
Fonts: Inter + Instrument Serif + JetBrains Mono

All three free. Google Fonts. Inter is not Geist — battle-tested since 2017, warm enough, carries no "AI product" smell. Instrument Serif has genuine character and the italic is gorgeous.

Dropped: Geist, Fraunces, GT Super, Söhne.

Fixed
One signature gesture, everywhere

Serif italic + a hand-drawn oxblood underline, used only on the page title greeting. Same gesture on every screen. Not a different quirk per surface.

Dropped: "One weird thing per screen."

Fixed
No handwritten font

Caveat, Homemade Apple, etc. all look cheap at scale and undermine credibility when a user is emailing a Goldman MD. We use Instrument Serif italic for "personal" moments instead.

Dropped: Script/handwriting display font.

Committed
Stationery, not collegiate or yearbook

Collegiate reads as school project. Yearbook reads as nostalgic. Stationery reads as "I take my professional communication seriously" — which is exactly the stance Offerloop should take.

Fixed
Keep Lucide. Use fewer icons.

Maintaining a custom icon set across 100+ components has no ROI. Instead: prefer text labels, keep icons only where they disambiguate (search, navigation, status). Same library, less of it.

Dropped: hand-drawn custom icons, printer's ornaments as icons.

Fixed
Paper texture: inline SVG noise, zero assets

An feTurbulence SVG inlined as a data URI, blended in multiply. Sub-1kb, sharp at any DPI, no loading, no files.

Dropped: PNG texture, heavy filters, radial-blob gradients.

The stance in one sentence

Offerloop isn't an AI tool that writes emails — it's a letterpress that helps a student write one good letter to someone they already share something with.

II · Principles

Four rules, revised for stationery.

Same principles as v1, reframed around the new direction. When in doubt, pick the one that feels more like a thank-you note and less like a dashboard.

i.
Paper first, pixels second

The UI has material. Cream paper, printed ink, inset hairlines. Nothing floats in a void.

ii.
One system, free to ship

Three free typefaces. One brand color. One accent. A 4px grid. Nothing that requires a contract or a licensing audit.

iii.
Italic for the human moment

Serif italic appears once per screen, always on something a person would say. Never for chrome, labels, or data.

iv.
Earn every emphasis

Bold, color, and ornament each pick one thing per screen. The oxblood underline is used exactly once. The navy button too.

III · Type

Inter for work. Instrument Serif for moments.

Two families. Inter does every piece of UI chrome, body, numeric, label — all of it. Instrument Serif appears on page titles, in callouts, and for the personal italic. JetBrains Mono handles metadata: timestamps, counts, breadcrumbs.

V · Signature gesture

The italic + underline, everywhere, consistently.

Exactly one rule: on every page, the title uses Instrument Serif italic with a single hand-drawn oxblood underline on the meaningful word. Same SVG, same color, same position. It shows up in product screens, marketing, onboarding, emails — the connecting thread.

Dashboard greeting
Burning the midnight oil, Deena.
Find screen
Who are you looking for today?
Outbox empty state
Nothing sent yet. That's okay.
Landing hero
Write the email your network will read.
Onboarding step
Let's find your alumni, first.
The underline, in case anyone asks

Inline SVG, oxblood stroke, painted as a background-image on the em. One declaration, no components, no assets. Reuses across product + marketing + emails. The only "quirk" we maintain — everything else is strict.

Before · v1
Warm but generic

v1's cream + Geist + Fraunces read like every Linear-adjacent startup. "Tasteful" but indistinguishable.

After · v2
A physical object

Cream with visible grain, an engraved seal, printer's ink, an italic greeting underlined by a hand. The screen is the letter.

Shippable
Zero licensing, minimal assets

Three free fonts. No custom icon set. One inline SVG for noise. One inline SVG for the underline. Total new asset weight: ~2kb.

VIII · Spreadsheets

Three spreadsheets. One pattern.

The user's saved records live in three lists: People, Companies, Hiring Managers. Same chrome, same gestures, same keyboard shortcuts — different columns. These are rosters, not pipelines. Email status lives in the Tracker, separately. A contact can sit here for years without ever being emailed.

Why three and not one

A person you know at McKinsey is different data from McKinsey itself (HQ, alumni count, industry), and different again from a hiring manager at McKinsey who's actively posting roles. Conflating them forces empty columns. Keeping them separate lets each view be dense and useful — and the repeated grammar (sticky header, mono labels, same row height, same tab bar) makes switching painless.

IX · My Network (click-through)

Clicking "My Network" opens the People spreadsheet.

No greeting, no underline gesture — the greeting lives only on Find (the front door). My Network is utilitarian: you came here to work. Breadcrumb does the labelling; tabs mirror Find's IA exactly (People / Companies / Hiring Managers). Find is the ocean. My Network is your Rolodex.

Flow

Sidebar: Find → My Network → Coffee Chats → Tracker → Job Board. Click "My Network" → People tab active by default → switch tabs in-place to Companies or Hiring Managers. Same URL root (/my-network), tab is a sub-route (/my-network/companies).

Decision
No greeting here

Greeting + italic underline belong only to Find. My Network is utilitarian — breadcrumb labels, tabs switch, work begins.

Pattern
Tabs mirror Find

People / Companies / Hiring Managers — exact same labels, order, and component as Find. Users learn once.

Symmetry
Ocean vs. Rolodex

Find searches the world. My Network browses your saved. Same three tabs, different scope. The symmetry is the whole point.

X · Rollout

Four steps. Low risk.

Order is deliberate: tokens before type, type before components, product before marketing.

01
Tokens

Replace index.css variables. Keep Tailwind config. Flag-gated.

02
Type

Remove Lora, DM Sans, Libre Baskerville, IBM Plex Mono, VanquishBold. Load Inter + Instrument Serif + JetBrains Mono.

03
Primitives

Rebuild Button, Input, Badge, Card, Tab, Row. Add the <PageTitle> component with the italic-underline gesture baked in.

04
Marketing

Landing inherits the system. The letterhead header at the top of this page is a candidate for the real landing hero.
