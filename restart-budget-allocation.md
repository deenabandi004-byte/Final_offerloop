# Restart Budget Allocation: $12,500

Cold-restart, founder-led, $12,500 grant budget. Compiled by Claude (Opus 4.7), May 2026.

The hard truth before allocations: you do not have a baseline conversion rate that holds. The old 13.4% paid conversion was from a different funnel under different conditions. **Treat every dollar of paid spend in the first 30 days as research, not acquisition.** The goal of month 1 is to learn what works. The goal of month 2 is to spend on what worked.

---

## The allocation philosophy

Three principles:

1. **Founder time is the cheapest input.** You have three founders comfortable on camera and one weak conversion baseline. Founder content is "free" (in dollars) and produces signal that paid ads can amplify later. Don't lead with paid.
2. **Hold dry powder.** The biggest cold-restart mistake is spending the whole grant in month 1 because you assumed your old conversion rate would hold. The CrowdStrike retention math (97% post-outage) does not transfer to a $1.6K MRR startup; expect more variance.
3. **Spend where it compounds.** Tooling that builds an email list, captures referrals, or improves your funnel pays dividends in month 2, 3, and beyond. Spending on a single one-off ad does not.

### Bucket 2: UGC creator content via Billo ($1,500 in Month 1)

**The play**: 10 Billo videos at $99 to $150 each (typical Billo rate per [Billo's pricing](https://billo.app/blog/billo-pricing-ugc-videos-cost/)). Each video is a different angle / hook / format. These become:
1. Native posts on Offerloop's TikTok account (alongside founder posts)
2. The creative library for TikTok Spark Ads
3. Bank for re-cuts

**Why Billo and not Insense at this scale**: Insense is $299/mo subscription plus creator payments ($1.5K to $2.5K for 10 videos according to [Hashmeta's comparison](https://hashmeta.com/blog/ugc-platforms-comparison-billo-vs-insense-vs-joinbrands-which-is-best-for-your-brand/)). Billo is per-video with no subscription, $99 to $150 each. At 10 videos in month 1, Billo is meaningfully cheaper.

**Creator brief**: each creator gets the same brief (a single recruiting pain point + a 4-beat hook structure from the `tiktok-cold-restart.md` post examples). Variations:
- 3 videos: "Rejection panic → cold email → callback flex" (Palmy formula)
- 3 videos: "3 worst cold email mistakes" listicle
- 2 videos: "I'm a [school] student and I used Offerloop to land [firm]"
- 2 videos: "Showing Offerloop on screen in 30 seconds"

**What NOT to spend on at this stage**: Finance / consulting niche micro-influencers on TikTok charge $1,000 to $3,000 per video at 10K followers ([Influencer Marketing Hub TikTok rates 2026](https://influencermarketinghub.com/tiktok-influencer-rates/), [Influee 2026 rates](https://influee.co/blog/tiktok-influencer-rates)) because finance is a high-CPM niche. **One $2,000 finance influencer video is more risky than 10 $150 Billo UGC videos for the same money**, because:
- A single influencer post lives or dies on one creator's audience match.
- 10 Billo creatives give you statistical signal on what hook works before you scale.
- A failed influencer post burns $2,000 and teaches you nothing transferable.

Hold the finance-niche micro-influencer play for Month 2 or 3, once you know which hook converts.

### Bucket 3: TikTok Spark Ads paid testing ($1,500 in Month 1)

**The play**: $50/day × 30 days = $1,500. Run 3 to 4 Spark Ads on top of your strongest organic founder posts and 2 to 3 of the Billo videos. Optimize for landing page views in week 1, then for signups in week 2 once the algorithm has data.

**Reference numbers**: TikTok's effective minimum for conversion optimization is $50/day per ad group ([Stackmatix 2026 TikTok ads guide](https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/tiktok-ads-minimum-daily-budget-2026)). Spark Ads "deliver 30 to 40% lower CPMs than standard In-Feed because organic content already carries social proof" ([same source](https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/tiktok-ads-minimum-daily-budget-2026)).

**What "credible signal" looks like at this spend**:
- 10K to 30K impressions per day (~$0.05 to $0.15 CPM range for college students)
- 100 to 300 link clicks per day
- 20 to 50 signups per day if conversion ratio is reasonable
- Cost per signup: $3 to $15 range = credible. Above $25 = wrong creative.

**What "no signal" looks like**: <50 impressions per day = the creative is failing the follower-first gate (`tiktok-cold-restart.md` Part 1). Don't burn budget; refresh creative.

**ASSUMPTION**: Spark Ads requires a TikTok Business account and creator partner permissions for each organic post you boost. Set up before Day 1, not during.

### Bucket 4: Meta Reels paid testing ($750 in Month 1)

**The play**: $25/day × 30 days = $750. Run 2 Reels-placement ads with the best 2 to 3 Billo creatives recut for IG Reels format.

**Why this is a smaller bet than TikTok**: Meta Reels has a higher floor for credible testing. The "50 conversion events per ad set per week" rule ([Stackmatix Meta budget 2026](https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/meta-ads-minimum-daily-budget-2026)) means if your CPA is $15, you need $750/week per ad set just to exit learning phase. At $25/day total you're below learning-phase threshold by design. You're using Meta as a credibility comparison signal, not as a primary acquisition channel in Month 1.

**Why test it anyway**: IG Reels has stronger conversion-tracking on landing pages than TikTok (Meta's pixel + Conversions API are more mature). If conversion economics look better on Meta than TikTok, that informs the Month 2 reallocation.

**What "credible signal" looks like**: Reels CPMs in the $7 to $12 range ([Digital Applied's Reels guide](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/instagram-reels-ads-2026-creative-strategy-guide)), CPC $0.40 to $1.50. If your CPC is below $1.00, the creative is working. If above $2.00, refresh.

**The play**: 2 in-person events. Use Offerloop's USC + Berkeley relationships.

**Targeting**: time tabling for high-foot-traffic moments. USC's Trousdale or Marshall lobby during career fair week. Berkeley's Upper Sproul during club week or RSO fair. Avoid finals weeks and early mornings.

**ROI math**: at $500 per event, if each event drives 50 signups, that's $10 per signup. Comparable to paid ads at this scale, but signups from in-person events convert at much higher rates. If conversion to paid is 5x higher than online (typical for in-person demos), effective CAC drops to ~$2 to $4. Best $/signup of any tactic in this allocation.

## The dry-powder structure

After Month 1, you have $7,000 left ($12,500 - $5,500). Hold it.

### Month 2 ($4,500): allocate based on Month 1 signal

The decision tree depends on what Month 1 showed:

**If TikTok Spark Ads converted at <$15 CPA**:
- Double TikTok spend: $3,000 in Month 2
- Hire 1 finance/consulting micro-influencer: $1,500 for one solid post + repost rights
- Keep tooling subscriptions: ~$100

**If Spark Ads underperformed (CPA >$25) but Billo creative drove organic engagement**:
- Cut Spark Ads to $500
- Double Billo creative volume: $2,500 for 16 to 20 new videos
- Test Meta Reels harder: $1,000
- Tooling: $500 (add maybe Klaviyo or upgrade to Customer.io if list is >2K)

**If everything paid underperformed and Bucket 5 (campus events) drove the most signups**:
- Cut all paid: $0
- 6 more campus events at $500 each: $3,000
- 2 club partnership sponsorships at $500 each: $1,000
- Tooling: $500

**If the founder content engine drove the most signups (no paid required)**:
- Skip paid entirely in Month 2
- Invest in production: 1 nicer camera or lapel mic for Nick, $300
- 2 freelance editors to help the founders cut TikToks faster: $2,000 for 30 days
- Reserve $2,000 of the Month 2 budget for Month 3

The framework: **Month 2 spend should mirror what Month 1 proved**. If Month 1 proved nothing, do not assume; cut spend and run more cheap experiments.

### Month 3+ ($2,500): true reserve

Hold for:
- A creator who blows up unexpectedly (boost that post immediately, $500 to $1,000)
- A school partnership that needs a $1,500 sponsorship (e.g., the USC IB Society annual conference)
- An emergency (the site has another outage and you need to extend Pro for affected users)
- An obvious win you didn't predict

Do not spend this proactively. Spending it on "more of what's working" is what Month 2 budget is for.

## What you are explicitly NOT spending on

These come up as plausible options and I'm telling you to skip:

1. **Influencer marketplaces with min-spend contracts** (Aspire, GRIN, etc.). Each requires $500 to $5K platform fees on top of creator costs. Skip until Month 3+.
2. **Google Search ads.** College students searching "how to write an IB cold email" exist, but the keyword competition is intense (career-services companies, education brands). Your CPC will be $3 to $8 and conversion will be weak. Skip.
3. **Reddit ads.** r/csmajors and r/consulting accept ads, but Reddit ad performance for consumer products targeting students is historically poor. Skip.
4. **LinkedIn ads.** Min spend is $10/day per campaign but LinkedIn CPMs are $30 to $80 (the highest of any platform). LinkedIn organic founder posts (free) will outperform LinkedIn ads at this scale.
5. **Snapchat / X / Pinterest ads.** Marginal ROI for ICP. Skip.
6. **Influencer marketing on Instagram (not TikTok).** IG influencer rates are higher per follower than TikTok and conversion is weaker for app installs. TikTok-first.
7. **A polished marketing site redesign.** Conversion site issues come up in Month 2 after you have funnel data, not on the assumption.
8. **A PR agency or "growth hacker" contractor.** Founder content is the play, not paid PR. Skip.
9. **Swag beyond stickers + tote bags.** Branded water bottles, hoodies, etc. are over-investment when you haven't validated which messaging converts.
10. **Domain or app rebranding.** Don't redesign the brand while the funnel is empty. Fix the funnel first.

## Credible signal thresholds (what tells you Month 1 worked)

**By end of Week 2 of Month 1**:
- TikTok Spark Ads CPA < $20 = working
- Meta Reels CPM < $12 = working
- At least 1 Billo video gets organic 25K views = creative is right
- 2 campus events drove 80+ signups combined = grassroots is right
- Loops.so welcome sequence has 30%+ open rate = funnel is right

**By end of Month 1**:
- 200+ new signups attributable to paid + UGC = green light Month 2
- Less than 100 new signups = the funnel itself is broken; do not double paid spend
- 5+ club partnerships locked = green light scale

## Methodology

Queries that produced the strongest sources:

1. `Billo Insense UGC creator content pricing startup small budget`
2. `TikTok Spark Ads minimum budget test small startup 2026`
3. `finance consulting TikTok micro influencer rates cost 10k followers`
4. `Meta Reels Instagram ads minimum spend test budget startup 2026`
5. `referral software SaaS Loops customer.io retention email startup pricing`

**What worked**: vendor pricing pages and 2026 cost guides from social-media-tooling vendors. Numbers like the $50/day TikTok minimum and the 50-conversions-per-ad-set Meta rule are cited consistently across multiple independent sources.

**What didn't work**: precise CAC benchmarks for "college students chasing IB" specifically. I had to use general TikTok / Meta benchmarks. **DATA THIN** for your exact ICP CAC. Treat the CPA thresholds above as a starting reasonableness test, not as targets.

**Sanitized narrative flag**: every UGC platform writes its own "how to allocate budget" content. Take vendor cost claims (e.g., "save 40% with Billo") with skepticism. Cross-source agreement on Billo's $99 base price was strong, though.

**ASSUMPTIONS**:
- ASSUMPTION: the $12.5K is fungible across paid ads, UGC, tooling, and events. If the grant has restrictions (e.g., must be spent at named vendors), the allocation will need to adjust.
- ASSUMPTION: founder time has zero direct dollar cost. If you'd value Nick / Sid / Rylan time at $50 to $100/hour, founder content has a real shadow cost; the allocation logic still holds but the "free" framing is loose.
- ASSUMPTION: Offerloop has tax-deductible / non-profit eligibility status if needed for sponsored campus events. Most universities require this for sponsorships.
