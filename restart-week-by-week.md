## The first 3 actions that spend budget, and why those 3 first

In execution order:

1. **Day 2 (Tuesday): Post 10 Billo creator briefs at $150 each (~$1,500).** Why first: Billo videos take 5 to 7 days to deliver. If you wait until Week 2 to commission them, you have no creative inventory when you want to start paid ad testing in Week 2 to 3. Order them in Week 1 so they arrive by Week 2 Monday.
2. **Day 3 (Wednesday): Subscribe to Loops.so paid tier (~$49).** Why second: only spend the $49 once you have actual signups coming in from the "we're back" announcement. Loops free tier handles small lists, but the campaign-blast to 1,027 signups on Day 3 is the moment paid features unlock value.
3. **Day 5 (Friday): Subscribe to Viral Loops referral ($49) + ship referral mechanic.** Why third: once new signups start arriving from the comeback announcement + TikTok #1, you want a referral mechanic in place so each new user can refer two friends for credits. Ship before the second wave of signups, not after.

Total Week 1 spend: ~$1,600 of the $5,500 Month 1 budget. The big paid-ad spend ($1,500 TikTok + $750 Meta) holds until Week 2 to 3, after Billo creative arrives and you have something credible to boost.

## North star metric for Week 6

**Primary**: **500 net-new signups in 42 days** (excluding any reactivated churners from the original 91).

**Secondary (paid validation)**: **30 net-new paid users in 42 days** (excluding any reactivated). At 6% conversion from 500 signups, this is in range. At your old 13.4% conversion, this is exceeded.

**Health (MRR)**: **$1,500 MRR by end of Week 6**. This brings you to ~90% of the pre-outage baseline ($1,665), counting both reactivated and net-new.

If you hit all three by Day 42, the restart is working and Month 3 is a scale problem, not a survival problem.

If you hit primary only: you have audience but conversion is weak. Investigate the product/pricing/onboarding before Month 3 paid spend.

If you hit secondary only: you have a small loyal cohort but distribution is broken. Investigate the top of the funnel.

If you hit neither: the product itself isn't pulling. Stop spending. Return to the PMF Engine survey and the concierge calls. Solve PMF before spending the rest of the grant.

## A note on tone for the entire 6 weeks

Across every channel and every post, hold the line that:
- You are three college students, named, building this.
- You went down. You came back. You are not pretending the gap didn't happen.
- The product is for the bottom 80% of college recruits, not the top 20% (the polarizing positioning from `marketing-research.md` Tier 3, B section).
- Founder identity is the moat. Sid, Nick, Rylan are not "founders of Offerloop," they are themselves, with public-facing personality.

The risk over 6 weeks is that the energy fades and the content drifts into AI-sloppy "founder Twitter" tone. The fix is the founders posting in their own voice, ideally typed (not scripted), often with screenshots, and always with one specific human detail.
