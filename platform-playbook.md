## Platform 1: X / Twitter

X is where founders earn distribution from other founders, operators, and investors. The audience is small but high-status. For Offerloop, X is the channel that builds credibility, attracts PR, and recruits ambassadors. It is **not** where most college students live, so do not measure X performance against signup volume. Measure it against credibility and inbound.

### Cluely (Roy Lee)

**The thesis** is stated explicitly in [Cluely's own blog post on virality](https://cluely.com/blog/virality) and in Roy's TechCrunch Disrupt 2025 statements: **controversial posts that produce strong negative reactions from about half the audience are the most likely to go viral.** Roy is on record: "if half the people in the world wouldn't feel very negatively about a post, it probably won't go viral" ([Daily.dev recap](https://app.daily.dev/posts/how-roy-lee-cluely-makes-everything-go-viral-on-x-ai-cluely-viral-roylee-tweet-6j6fniogt)).

**The distribution machine**:
- 60+ content creators and 700+ video editors on the team
- An SF house that doubles as content studio
- "Engineers just cannot make good content" - explicit team strategy from Roy at Disrupt
- Every team member produces content: skits, demos, podcasts

**The launch tweet** (April 20, 2025): [Roy on X](https://x.com/im_roy_lee/status/1914061483149001132), "Cluely is out. cheat on everything." Result: ~70K signups week one, $5.3M seed within 24 hours.

**Result**: $0 to $5M ARR in 3 months ([PMF Show interview with Roy](https://www.pmf.show/he-got-kicked-out-of-harvard-then-grew-to-5m-arr-in-just-3-months-roy-lee-co-founder-of-cluely/)).

**Offerloop translation**: Do not try to replicate Cluely's 60-creator/700-editor pipeline. That is a $1M+/year content operation. **Steal the polarization principle, not the production budget.** Nick, once per week, should publish a single X post or thread that ~half of college recruiting Twitter would disagree with. Examples:
- "OCR is a scam for the bottom 80% of college students. Here's the math."
- "Career centers tell you to network. They don't tell you what to say. Here's the cold email I sent 47 Goldman analysts."
- "If your school isn't a target, the median behavior is to give up. The cold-email path has a higher hit rate than OCR."

Each is true, falsifiable, and will draw quote-tweets from career-services professionals and traditional networking advocates.

### Mercury (Immad Akhund)

Mercury's first-customer engine for the first two months after launch was **founder Twitter**, full stop ([First Round podcast with Immad](https://review.firstround.com/podcast/lessons-from-mercury/)). The mechanism:

- Immad raised from 60 angel investors in the initial round
- **Two of those angels tweeted Mercury to their followings**: Justin Kan (Twitch) and Elad Gil (Mixer Labs)
- "Those two tweets made quite a big difference in not just spreading the word but also making people feel like they could trust us"
- Mercury now valued >$1.6B ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/mercury-3))

**Offerloop translation**: You do not have angels with 100K+ X followings (yet). But you have **two named credibility markers**: 4th of ~250 at USC NVSC, 3rd at UC LAUNCH Spring 2026. Those competitions have judges, advisors, and prior winners with X followings. DM 5 of them this week and ask if they would tweet or quote-tweet a single product update. The Mercury playbook works with a 5K-following angel just as well as a 500K one, because the trust transfer is what matters.

### Linear (Karri Saarinen)

The [@linear](https://twitter.com/linear) account in 2019 to 2020 ran as **a live public changelog**. Not a marketing account. Every ship became a tweet with a screenshot. Karri's personal @karrisaarinen ran a parallel "design first principles" diary. Sequoia found Linear because "people she trusts in her Twitter network were hyped about Linear" ([Growth Letter on Linear](https://www.growth-letter.com/p/this-startup-had-10000-people-on)).

**Offerloop translation**: The @offerloop brand account should ship a public update every time you ship a feature. Format: "[Feature name]. [One screenshot.] [One sentence on why.]" That's the entire tweet. Sid (CTO) is the natural author. Cadence: at least 2x/week. The audience is small but it compounds, and it's the kind of post that VCs and operators screenshot and share to *their* networks.

### Granola (Chris Pedregal)

Pedregal posts on [@cjpedregal](https://x.com/cjpedregal) ~daily but quietly. The amplification is done by high-status operators (Dan Shipper of Every, Matt Turck, Lenny Rachitsky) quoting him. Three tonal features of Pedregal's posts:

1. Substantive (a real ship, a real number, a contrarian POV)
2. Calm voice (no exclamation marks, no caps lock, no emoji)
3. Replies to users by name when they post about Granola

**Offerloop translation**: Pedregal is the canonical example of "build in public quietly." If Nick wants to be the polarizing voice and Sid wants to be the technical voice, this is Sid's mode: substantive, calm, replies-to-users. Each founder owns one register; do not have all three in the same tonal mode.

### Sahil Lavingia (Gumroad)

The Lavingia mechanic: **post the financials monthly**. Public roadmap. Public board meetings. The thing people miss about Lavingia is the [Holloway-cited principle](https://www.holloway.com/g/using-twitter): "Building in public isn't important, what's important is succeeding in public." Sahil's metric-sharing only works because Gumroad is succeeding. **Posting losses for a year alienates the audience**; posting wins for a year builds it.

**Offerloop translation**: As MRR, signups, and conversion grow, post the numbers. Once per month, one tweet, one number, one screenshot. **Wait until you have ~10 weeks of growth to start this, not week 1**, so the curve is positive when you begin.

### X synthesis for Offerloop

| Owner | Mode | Cadence | One example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nick (CEO) | Polarizing POV in the recruiting niche | 1 post/week + daily quote-tweets | "OCR is a scam for the bottom 80%" |
| Sid (CTO) | Build-in-public technical, Granola-tone | 2 posts/week (Tue + Thu) | New feature ship + 1 screenshot + 1 sentence |
| @offerloop brand | Live changelog | 2 posts/week | "We shipped X today. Here's what it does." |
| All three | Reply to and quote-tweet operators in the niche | Daily | Founders who follow Wonsulting, Teal, career-services pros |

**X is not where you measure signups. X is where you build the inbound that lets the other platforms work.**

## Platform 2: LinkedIn

LinkedIn is where Offerloop's audience actually congregates. College students researching IB / MBB / tech recruiting are on LinkedIn in volume. The platform's 2026 algorithm rewards two specific things: long carousel posts and founder-personal stories. Both are achievable at zero budget.

### Wonsulting (Jonathan Javier)

Wonsulting is **the closest direct analog to Offerloop's positioning**. Jonathan Javier turned a LinkedIn personal brand into a career-coaching company. "Helping an underdog land a job a day" is his tagline, recognizable across the platform. Forbes 30 Under 30, with named credibility from Cisco, Google, Snap ([Jonathan's LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jonathan-wonsulting/)).

**LinkedIn was the starting platform**, before Wonsulting's TikTok push. Jonathan's playbook there was three things:

1. **Personal storytelling**: first-person narratives about Cisco, Google, Snap experiences, with the lesson at the end
2. **Free resume reviews weekly**: a recurring CTA that builds inbound DMs
3. **One named beneficiary per post**: "I helped Maya land a Goldman SA. Here's exactly what we did." Real stories, real names (with permission), real outcomes.

**Offerloop translation**: Nick should publish 1 "I helped [named user] land [named firm]" post per week starting the moment he has the first one. The post is structured:
- Hook: "Last month, a USC sophomore got rejected from 14 IB SA programs."
- Story: 5 to 7 lines of what happened, what they tried, what didn't work.
- Turning point: "Then we used Offerloop to send 53 cold emails using this framework."
- Outcome: "She got 4 Superdays. Here's the framework."
- Carousel: 7 slides of the actual framework.
- CTA: "If you're in this situation, comment 'cold' and I'll send you the template." (No bait, real CTA.)

### Series (Nathaneo Johnson, Yale)

Series is the most recent data point and the clearest "LinkedIn launch video" case study. Co-founder Nathaneo Johnson posted a **trailer video on LinkedIn** about Series, it went viral in the college entrepreneurial community, and the company raised $3.1M in 14 days ([AfroTech](https://afrotech.com/yale-university-students-raised-3-1m-in-14-days), [TechCrunch April 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/24/two-college-kids-raise-a-5-1-million-pre-seed-to-build-an-ai-social-network-in-imessage/)).

**The trailer mechanic**: short (~60 seconds), founder on camera, real product demo, no produced gloss. Series later upgraded to "reality-TV-style series" and "hiring a robot to accompany the founders" as growth stunts ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/24/two-college-kids-raise-a-5-1-million-pre-seed-to-build-an-ai-social-network-in-imessage/)). The first viral hit was just the trailer.

**Offerloop translation**: Make a 60-second product trailer. Nick on camera, real screen recording of Offerloop in use, no production. Post it on LinkedIn under Nick's personal account with a hook like: "I'm 21. I'm the CEO of Offerloop. This is what we just shipped." This is the single highest-variance LinkedIn play in the playbook: variance is high because most product trailers flop, but the upside is a Series-scale moment.

### Teal HQ

Teal's [TikTok account](https://www.tiktok.com/@teal_hq) has 108K+ followers and 1.4M+ likes, but its LinkedIn presence is equally important: it's where they capture credibility and partnerships. Teal's LinkedIn content format:

- Resume tips with screenshots
- "Job search tips" carousel posts
- Cross-posting their TikTok videos as native LinkedIn videos

**Offerloop translation**: Repurpose every TikTok directly to LinkedIn native video. Algorithm differences are real but the content cross-posts well. **One piece of content, three platforms**.

### What the 2026 LinkedIn algorithm rewards (data, not opinion)

From [Linkboost 2026 algorithm guide](https://blog.linkboost.co/how-to-go-viral-on-linkedin-2026/), [Use Visuals 2026 carousel data](https://usevisuals.com/blog/linkedin-carousel-engagement-statistics-2026), and [Connect Safely 2026 examples](https://connectsafely.ai/articles/linkedin-post-examples-high-engagement-2026):

1. **Carousels (PDF documents) get 3.4x more reach and 2.1x more engagement** than single-image posts.
2. **7-slide carousels perform 18% better** than any other carousel length.
3. **First 210 characters are the hook** (everything after is truncated until "see more").
4. **Drop-off between slide 1 and slide 2 is the biggest engagement killer**. Use a pattern interrupt on slide 2: different background color, bold stat, controversial line.
5. **External links in the post body trigger a ~60% reach penalty**. Put links in the first comment.
6. **Dwell time matters more than likes**. Carousels force swipes, which boost dwell.
7. **Avoid "Comment YES" engagement bait**. Algorithm explicitly down-ranks.
8. **Best post times for B2B**: Tuesday to Thursday, 7 AM to 9 AM local time of the audience.

### LinkedIn synthesis for Offerloop

| Format | Cadence | Owner | Topic |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7-slide carousel | 2x/week | Nick | "What 47 Goldman cold emails taught me" type frameworks |
| Personal story post (text + 1 image) | 1x/week | Nick | "I helped [named user] land [named firm]" |
| Product trailer (video) | Monthly | Nick | The Series-style 60-second trailer |
| Build-in-public technical post | 1x/week | Sid | Behind-the-scenes feature ships |
| Comment + reply on big career creators' posts | Daily | All three | Wonsulting, Teal, Jonathan Javier, Madeline Mann, career-services pros |

**LinkedIn is where the most direct ICP-to-signup conversion will happen for Offerloop.** Prioritize.

## Platform 3: TikTok

TikTok is the highest-volume audience for Offerloop's ICP. It's also the platform with the highest variance and the harshest 2026 algorithm. Multiple companies have built consumer empires there from zero; the playbooks are well-documented.

### Cal AI (Zach Yadegari)

Cal AI is in `marketing-research.md` already as a paid-influencer playbook. The TikTok-organic side of the story is separately instructive ([Growwithplutus Cal AI deep dive](https://growwithplutus.com/blog/cal-ai-app-tiktok-strategy)):

- **12 TikTok accounts** running in parallel (multi-account strategy)
- **1,000+ videos** across accounts
- Main founder account: 281 videos, 2.1M total views
- Three content modes: talking-head demos, accuracy tests (showing AI scan vs. manual logging), weekly nutrition tip series
- **Algorithm-trained**: the team built a fresh For You feed by only engaging with fitness creators until the algorithm fed back relevant creators to evaluate

**Offerloop translation**:
- Start with **one** founder account, not 12. You don't have the content volume to feed 12. After 60 days and signal, consider 2 to 3 accounts (e.g., @offerloop_main for product, @nick_offerloop for founder voice, @offerloop_ib for IB-specific content).
- Cal AI's three modes translate directly: **product demo + accuracy / proof test + weekly recurring series**. For Offerloop:
  - Product demo: 30-second screen recording of cold email being generated and sent
  - Proof test: "I sent the AI-generated email and the manually-written one. Here's which got replies."
  - Weekly recurring: "Cold Email of the Week" teardown, every Monday

### Wonsulting (TikTok playbook)

Wonsulting reached **575K TikTok followers in 4 months**, plus 30M+ monthly impressions cross-platform ([Wonsulting's own writeup](https://www.wonsulting.com/blog/how-wonsulting-tiktok-achieved-500k-followers-within-4-months)). Specifics from that blog:

- **Initial burst**: 3 to 5 videos per day for the first 2 weeks
- **Sustainable cadence**: 1 to 2 videos per day during peak hours (10 AM and 8 PM PT)
- **Monthly output**: 30 to 60 videos
- **First viral video**: a personal story about Jonathan's managers at Kohl's, 815K+ views
- **Highest-impact format**: stitches and duets of job-seekers. One example: a stitch helping a job-seeker named Sam got 2M+ views and led to her landing a job a week later.
- **Brand quirk**: a "Baby Grogu pen" used as a pointer in tip videos. The prop became recognizable.

**Offerloop translation**:
- **Initial burst is the most important variable**: 3 to 5 videos per day for the first 2 weeks is the play. Yes, it's a lot. Don't skip this.
- **Stitches and duets are your highest-leverage format**: find TikToks of college students venting about being rejected from IB / MBB / tech recruiting and stitch them with "Here's exactly what I would have done." Each stitch is faster to make than an original video and rides on the original's traffic.
- **Steal the prop idea**: pick one recurring visual element (a specific notebook, a specific mug, a sign) that becomes recognizable across all your videos.

### Final Round AI (Michael Guan)

Final Round AI grew from zero to 10M+ users with **240K Instagram followers and a strong TikTok presence**. The founder is on record about the origin: "At first, it was just me taking videos on Instagram and TikTok. But people resonated with it" ([Founders Press profile](https://thefounderspress.com/how-final-round-ai-is-reshaping-job-interviews/)).

Two structural moves Final Round AI made:

1. **Founder-shot phone-video content** as the initial engine
2. An **affiliate / influencer program with 25% commission** ([Final Round AI Influencer Program](https://www.finalroundai.com/influencer-program)) once the format was proven

**Offerloop translation**: Nick / Sid / Rylan phone-shoot the first 30 to 60 videos. **Do not hire UGC creators yet**. Founder-shot, even if rough, outperforms UGC for cold-start because the audience credibility is "this is the actual founder." Once one founder video crosses 100K views, stand up an Offerloop affiliate program (25% rev share like Final Round AI; or free Elite for life as the credit equivalent).

### Erika Kullberg (the cleanest hook formula)

Erika is not in the recruiting niche, but her **hook formula is the most documented and replicable** in TikTok-creator literature. From [Growth in Reverse](https://growthinreverse.com/erika-kullberg/) and [Startup Spells](https://startupspells.com/p/exposing-erika-kullbergs-21-million-followers-growth-strategy):

The 6-step hook formula:
1. **Lead with the outcome** in the first 5 seconds. "Hi, my shoe has a tear, can I get a new pair?" (75.4M views)
2. **Two-character skit**: makes viewers more engaged than direct-to-camera
3. **Rejection moment**: brand says no, creating tension
4. **Whisper to camera**: "Shhhh, they don't know this hack"
5. **Explain the loophole** clearly
6. **Direct CTA to follow** (she literally points at the follow button)

Video length: **30 seconds or less**. Result: 0 to 1M followers in a week after implementing the formula. 8M of her current 21M followers came from **repurposing the same content to Instagram**.

**Offerloop translation**: This formula maps almost directly to Offerloop's domain. Two-character skit with Nick + Rylan:
- Beat 1 (0 to 5 sec): "Hi, I got rejected from every IB OCR firm."
- Beat 2: Rylan as "the firm": "Unfortunately, you don't fit our pipeline this cycle." (rejection moment)
- Beat 3: Nick to camera, whispered: "Shhhh, they don't know this works." (curiosity hook)
- Beat 4: Show the cold email framework on screen. (the "loophole")
- Beat 5: "She got 3 SA offers. Comment 'cold' for the template." (CTA)

Total runtime: 28 to 35 seconds. Drop the literal "Shhhh" if it feels off-brand; the *mechanic* is the curiosity gap.

### Madeline Mann (Self Made Millennial)

[Self Made Millennial](https://www.tiktok.com/@selfmademillennial) is **the #2 career-focused TikTok channel globally** (behind Gary Vaynerchuk). Madeline is an HR leader by background. Her format is recurring series (e.g., "Today I'll tell you what HR REALLY thinks about [X]"), tightly niche-anchored, with the HR-insider POV as her credibility marker.

**Offerloop translation**: You have a credibility marker that Madeline doesn't: you're college students yourselves. Each founder's TikTok bio should lead with one specific authority claim:
- Nick: "21yo CEO of Offerloop. Helped 91 students land IB/MBB SAs."
- Sid: "21yo CTO. Building Offerloop. Sent 800+ cold emails before launching."
- Rylan: "21yo CMO. Tracking 1,200 OCR applications across target schools."

The numbers don't have to be huge. They have to be specific and true. "Helped 91 students" is more credible than "thousands."

### Palmy / Palmify

In `marketing-research.md` already as the closest analog. Imperial College London students, 280K users, 50M+ organic TikTok views. Their hook formula was packaged and handed to ~20 student creators who all posted under the same structure:

**Exam panic → AI cheat code → flex result**

For Offerloop the direct mapping is:

**Rejection panic → cold email framework → callback flex**

This is the same template as the Erika two-character skit above, just packaged for distribution to a roster of student creators in months 2 and 3. Start with the founders; recruit students once the format is proven.

### What the 2026 TikTok algorithm rewards

From [Loomly 2026](https://www.loomly.com/blog/tiktok-algorithm), [Socialync 2026](https://www.socialync.io/blog/tiktok-algorithm-2026-what-works-now), [Ampfluence 2026](https://www.ampfluence.com/how-the-tiktok-algorithm-works-in-2026-and-how-to-beat-it-for-more-reach/):

- **Hook in 1.7 seconds**. Average decision time to scroll or watch.
- **70% completion rate threshold** for viral candidacy (up from 50% in 2024)
- **Reply to comments in the first hour** to boost the algorithm signal
- **3 to 5 posts per week minimum**. Sub-1x/week = lose algorithmic momentum.
- **Niche consistency**: the algorithm needs niche signals to categorize the account. Don't switch from recruiting content to dance content.
- **Audio choice**: original audio or quiet niche-relevant audio. Trending sounds confuse the algorithm if they don't match the niche.

### TikTok synthesis for Offerloop

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|

## Platform 4: Instagram

Instagram is the cheapest distribution multiplier you have because **the content is the same as TikTok**. The platforms reward similar formats but Instagram has a more mature ads ecosystem (Meta pixel + Conversions API) and stronger carousel performance.

### Final Round AI on Instagram (the cleanest example)

Final Round AI [@finalround_ai](https://www.instagram.com/finalround_ai/) has 240K Instagram followers, built primarily by **re-uploading TikTok content as Reels**. The founder Michael Guan's quote ("At first, it was just me taking videos on Instagram and TikTok") confirms the cross-platform-as-default behavior.

**Offerloop translation**: 100% of TikTok content becomes Instagram Reels. Same video, vertical 9:16, no need to recut. Erika Kullberg's data confirms this: **8M of her 21M followers came from Instagram alone**, primarily from repurposing.

### Carousels as the IG-specific play

Reels are the cross-platform format. Carousels are Instagram-native and the format that **outperforms image posts ~15:1 for engagement**. From [Verlynk 2025](https://verlynk.com/blog/how-to-go-viral-on-instagram-in-2025-proven-reels-strategy-for-explosive-growth) and [Postnitro 2025](https://postnitro.ai/blog/post/viral-instagram-carousels-strategies-2025):

- **Carousel posts: 10.15% average engagement rate** (2025)
- **Reels: 1.23% average engagement** (still beats image posts at 0.70%)
- **Carousels up to 20 images**. The optimal sweet spot is 7 to 10.
- **30-90 second Reels**. 7 to 30 second Reels have higher completion rates for virality.
- **Best post times (Reels)**: Tuesday to Friday, 11 AM to 1 PM or 5 PM to 7 PM.
- **Watch time is the #1 ranking factor** (Adam Mosseri, January 2025).

### Career creators on Instagram

Teal HQ, Madeline Mann, Erika Kullberg all have material Instagram presences. The Instagram-specific moves are:

1. **Convert LinkedIn carousels directly to Instagram carousels.** Same 7-slide format. Same hook in slide 1.
2. **Use Stories for behind-the-scenes** founder content. Story posts don't compete with Reels for algorithmic distribution.
3. **Pin the top 3 Reels to the profile grid**. Profile grid is the first thing a new follower sees.

### Instagram synthesis for Offerloop

| Format | Source | Cadence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reels | Repurposed TikToks, 100% reuse | 3 to 5/week | Same video, vertical, no recut |
| Carousels | Converted LinkedIn carousels | 2/week | 7-slide format, pattern interrupt slide 2 |
| Stories | Founder behind-the-scenes | Daily | Office life, ships, user wins |
| DMs | Inbound from Reels | All inbound | Nick personally responds for first 30 days |

**Instagram is the multiplier. You spend zero additional content time and get a second audience layer.** Skipping it would be giving up free reach.

## Cross-platform synthesis for Offerloop

The mistake every cold-start founder makes is treating each platform as a separate effort. The companies that broke out (Final Round AI, Wonsulting, Cal AI, Erika Kullberg) **made one piece of content per day and distributed it on every platform with platform-appropriate framing**.

### The content engine

| Step | Time cost | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Step 1**: One founder shoots one ~35-second video per day. Phone, vertical, no edit. | 15 minutes | 1 raw video |
| **Step 2**: Light CapCut edit (captions, cuts, music). | 20 minutes | 1 polished video |
| **Step 3**: Upload natively to TikTok. | 5 minutes | TikTok post live |
| **Step 4**: Re-upload exact same video to Instagram Reels. | 3 minutes | Reels post live |
| **Step 5**: Convert the video's core idea to a 7-slide LinkedIn carousel (use Canva). | 30 minutes | Carousel ready |
| **Step 6**: Post carousel on LinkedIn (Nick's personal account). | 5 minutes | LinkedIn post live |
| **Step 7**: Convert the video's core point to a short X thread (3 to 5 tweets). | 10 minutes | X thread ready |
| **Step 8**: Post thread on X (Sid's or Nick's personal account, alternating). | 3 minutes | X thread live |
| **Total** | **~90 min/day** | 1 piece of content on 4 platforms |

90 minutes of one founder's day produces 4 platform-native posts. Two founders alternating cuts this to 45 minutes per founder per day.

### Founder lane assignment

| Founder | Primary platform | Secondary | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nick** | LinkedIn | TikTok (on camera) | Polarizing POV + personal stories. Wonsulting-style. |
| **Sid** | X | LinkedIn (technical posts) | Build-in-public. Granola tone. |
| **Rylan** | TikTok ops | Instagram | Behind-the-scenes, growth ops, occasional on-camera |

Nick is the public face. Sid is the technical brain. Rylan is the operator. Each owns one platform primarily so accountability is clear.

### Numbers to hit

These are credible-success thresholds, drawn from the analog companies:

**30-day**:
- 1 LinkedIn post above 10K views (Series + Wonsulting pattern)
- 1 TikTok video above 25K views (early Cal AI / Wonsulting pattern)
- 1 X post quoted by an operator with a 5K+ following (Linear / Mercury pattern)
- 500+ followers added across all four platforms combined

**60-day**:
- 1 LinkedIn post above 50K views
- 1 TikTok video above 100K views
- An inbound DM from at least one career-creator or VC
- 2,000+ followers added across all four platforms combined

**90-day**:
- 1 LinkedIn post above 100K views or 1 TikTok video above 500K views (the "we have a hit" threshold)
- 5,000+ followers added across all four platforms combined
- A documentable signup-to-source attribution showing at least 30% of new signups come from your own content

These are bar thresholds, not targets. Hitting them = on track. Missing them by 50% = the format is wrong (rewrite the hook). Missing them by 90% = the platform is wrong for the niche (re-allocate effort).

### What you skip

To stay focused, skip these in the first 90 days:

- **YouTube long-form**. Too high production cost for the early-stage ROI. Come back to it once you have 60+ TikToks worth of content to compile.
- **Threads (Meta's X clone)**. Audience overlap is too thin and the algorithm is unproven for career-niche content.
- **BeReal, Lemon8, Bluesky, the rest**. Distraction. Re-evaluate in Q4.
- **Discord**. Defer until you have an audience to invite. See `first-1000-strategy.md` Tactic 7 for the longer reasoning if you still want it.
- **Reddit as a primary channel**. High variance, high ban risk. One value-first post per month max.

### One sentence that summarizes the playbook

You make one ~35-second founder-shot video per day, plus one 7-slide LinkedIn carousel per week, and you distribute both natively to TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, and X with platform-appropriate framing, **for 90 straight days**.

That's the entire content strategy. Everything else is amplification.
